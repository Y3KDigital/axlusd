# FREEZE + CLAWBACK POLICY (ALXUSD)

**Purpose:** Define when and how the Issuer freezes or claws back balances, consistent with institutional compliance expectations.

---

## I. POLICY PRINCIPLES

- Freeze/clawback exists to support compliance and enforce contracts.
- It is not discretionary “control for convenience.”
- All actions must be logged and reproducible.

---

## II. FREEZE

### Default State
- Trustlines are frozen by default (DefaultFreeze).
- Unfreeze occurs only after onboarding approval.

### Freeze Triggers
- sanctions screening match
- KYC expiration
- suspected fraud
- breach of agreement
- court order
- lender enforcement action

### Freeze Procedure
1. Compliance issues freeze instruction (ticket ID).
2. Issuer executes freeze action.
3. Program administrator records tx hash and reason.
4. Notify holder per agreement terms.

---

## III. CLAWBACK

### When Clawback Is Permitted
Clawback may occur only when:
- required by law/regulator/court
- explicitly permitted by holder agreement
- required to unwind a mistaken issuance

### Clawback Procedure
1. Legal/compliance confirms clawback authority.
2. Issuer executes clawback transaction.
3. Record tx hash and legal basis.
4. Update holder register and accounting.

### Holder Rights
Clawback does not remove contractual rights; disputes are resolved under governing law and agreements.

---

## IV. DISCLOSURES

All holders must receive:
- notice that freeze and clawback are enabled
- conditions under which they may be used
- remedies and dispute process
