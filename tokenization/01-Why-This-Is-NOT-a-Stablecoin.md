# WHY ALXUSD IS NOT A STABLECOIN

**Purpose:** Prevent regulatory misclassification and market confusion.

---

## I. DEFINITIONS

A “stablecoin” commonly implies:
- retail distribution
- on-demand redemption at par
- broad permissionless transferability
- reserves that are liquid and cash-like

`ALXUSD` is a **permissioned IOU** and does not make these promises.

---

## II. WHAT ALXUSD IS

- A claim on a specific issuer (SPV)
- Permissioned via trustlines and authorization
- Backed by a custodied gemstone collateral base
- Redeemable financially under contract-defined terms

---

## III. WHAT ALXUSD IS NOT

- Not a retail payment stablecoin
- Not redeemable on demand
- Not supported by AMM liquidity
- Not permissionless
- Not marketed as cash equivalent

---

## IV. REQUIRED DISCLOSURES

All counterparties must acknowledge:
- illiquidity of gemstone collateral
- valuation is not guaranteed liquidation value
- redemption timing depends on contract terms
- freeze/clawback can be used under defined conditions

---

## V. SAFE LANGUAGE

Use:
- “permissioned IOU”
- “issuer claim instrument”
- “asset-backed credit instrument”

Avoid:
- “stablecoin”
- “bank-grade dollar token”
- “cash equivalent”
